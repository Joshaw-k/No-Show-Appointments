Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, i want to find out some of the factor that have might cause patients to miss their appointments. Patient no-shows are long-standing issues affecting resource utilization and posing risks to the quality of healthcare services. They also lead to loss of anticipated revenue, particularly in services where resources are expensive and in great demand.

Dataset Overview¶

The Medical Appointments No-Show data consists of 110527 appointments and 14 features('PatientId', 'AppointmentID', 'Gender', 'ScheduledDay','AppointmentDay', 'Age', 'Neighbourhood', 'Scholarship', 'Hipertension','Diabetes', 'Alcoholism', 'Handcap', 'SMS_received', 'No-show'), after data cleaning the dataset was transformed to 110475 observations and 20 features(additional features: 'Scheduleddate', 'Appointmentday', 'Appointmentdate', 'AwaitingDays', 'Awaitingtime', 'Age_group').

Distribution Of Age¶

This distribution is bimodal with kids the age of 5 or less having the highest number of appointments followed by adults with the age 50 to 55

Proportion of No-Show Appointments¶

About 20% of appointments were missed which is quite a large number.Patients' failure to attend medical appointments saps resources and waste every business's most valued asset, "time.". When patients miss their medical appointment, the implications are detrimental to your practice and the patient.

Distribution of Age Groups and No-Show Rate¶

So out of the 20% of No-Show appointments in the dataset Adults had about 11.52% No-Show rate which is more than the total No-Show rate of the other age groups

Distribution of Health conditions and No-Show rate¶

Hypersion patients have the highest No-Show rate of all the Health related conditions with a No-Show rate of 3.41% and Handicap seeming to have the least No-Show rate

Distribution of Types of conditions and No-Show rate¶

Even after receiving text messages, patients on a scholarship have a No-Show rate which is far less than half the No-Show rate of patients with SMS received

Distribution of AwaitingDays and No-Show rate¶

Patients that have an awaiting time of a month have the highest No-Show rate, patients with an awaiting time of the same day have a low No-Show rate compared to awaiting time of a month

Distribution of Appointmentday and No-Show rate¶

From assessment the No-Show rate is higher before midweek and lower after midweek maybe due to the intensity of work which tends to be at the beginning of the week
